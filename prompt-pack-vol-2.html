Prompt Chaining & Workflow Systems
CAT 01
⚡ Multi-Step AI Workflows
Chain prompts together to build automated content pipelines — no coding required
Prompt 01 · Content Pipeline Builder
Full Content Repurposing Chain
I'm going to give you a YouTube video topic. Your job is to create a complete content chain: (1) a 1,500-word video script outline, (2) a 60-second Short script from the hook only, (3) 5 Twitter/X threads from key points, (4) a LinkedIn post from the conclusion, (5) an email subject line + 3-sentence teaser. Topic: [YOUR TOPIC]. Start with the script outline.
Run each step separately for best results. Feed step 1's output into step 2 as context.
Prompt 02 · Offer Build Chain
Digital Product Creation Pipeline
Help me build a digital product from scratch using this chain: Step 1 — Validate the idea: Does [PRODUCT IDEA] solve a real pain point for [TARGET AUDIENCE]? Step 2 — Name it: Give me 5 product name options. Step 3 — Outline it: Create a full table of contents. Step 4 — Price it: What's the right price point and why? Run each step, then wait for my approval before continuing.
Use this to move from "idea" to "ready to sell" in under 2 hours.
Prompt 03 · Loop Engineering
The Feedback Loop Prompt
I'm going to share a piece of content I created. Your job: (1) Rate it 1–10 on hook strength, clarity, and CTA, (2) Identify the single weakest element, (3) Rewrite only that element — don't touch the rest, (4) Explain why your version is stronger. Here's the content: [PASTE YOUR CONTENT]
Run this on every piece of content before publishing. The single-element rewrite keeps your voice intact.
Prompt 04 · Batch Creation
30 Days of Content in One Prompt
Create a 30-day content plan for a [NICHE] creator on YouTube. Format: Day number · Content type (Long-form/Short/Community post) · Topic title · Primary keyword · Hook line. Make 20 long-form, 8 Shorts, 2 community polls. Focus on topical authority — cluster related topics in the same week.
Batch this once per month. Export the table and drop it into your content calendar.
Prompt 05 · Master Workflow
The CFS Content Operating System
You are my AI content strategist. My channel is about [NICHE]. My target viewer is [AUDIENCE]. My income goal is [GOAL]. Each week I'll give you a topic and you'll produce: a YouTube title (3 options), thumbnail text (under 5 words), full video script outline (hook + 3 main points + CTA), one Short script, and an email to my list announcing the video. Confirm you understand and ask me for this week's topic.
Save this as a custom instruction or pin it at the start of every new chat. This is your weekly operating system.
⚡
// PRO TIP — CHAIN LIKE A PRO
The secret to advanced prompting isn't longer prompts — it's connected prompts. End each prompt with "Wait for my input before continuing." Then feed the output of step 1 as context into step 2. This keeps AI focused and prevents hallucination drift across long outputs.

Client-Facing Copy & Pitch Frameworks
CAT 02
💼 Get Paid as a Creator
Proposals, pitches, and copy that converts browsers into buyers
Prompt 06 · Brand Deal Pitch
Cold Outreach to Brands
Write a cold outreach email from a YouTube creator pitching a brand deal. Creator: [YOUR STATS/NICHE]. Brand: [BRAND NAME + PRODUCT]. Make it: under 150 words, lead with what's in it for them (not your stats), include one specific idea for how their product fits my content, end with a soft CTA to a 15-min call. No fluff. No "I hope this email finds you well."
Personalize the "specific idea" line for every brand. That one line is what gets replies.
Prompt 07 · Freelance Proposal
AI-Powered Service Proposal
Write a freelance service proposal for a client who needs [SERVICE TYPE]. Include: a one-paragraph problem statement (using their language), 3 deliverables with clear scope, a timeline, a price anchor with 2 tiers (standard and premium), and a risk reversal statement. Tone: confident, professional, no jargon. Client's industry: [INDUSTRY].
The two-tier pricing anchor makes the premium feel like a deal. Always include it.
Prompt 08 · Sales Page Copy
High-Converting Sales Page Structure
Write a sales page for a digital product using this structure: (1) Headline — outcome-focused, (2) Subheadline — who it's for, (3) Problem section — agitate the pain, (4) Solution — introduce the product, (5) What's inside — bullet list, (6) Social proof placeholder, (7) Price + value stack, (8) Guarantee, (9) CTA. Product: [PRODUCT NAME + DESCRIPTION]. Price: [PRICE]. Audience: [WHO IT'S FOR].
Use this for Gumroad, Payhip, or your own site. The value stack section alone can justify 3x your price.
Prompt 09 · Testimonial Mining
Turn Reviews Into Sales Copy
I'm going to paste 5 customer reviews for my product. Your job: (1) Identify the 3 strongest emotional phrases, (2) Rewrite them as bullet points for a sales page (keep the customer's words as close as possible), (3) Write a headline using the most repeated outcome across the reviews. Here are the reviews: [PASTE REVIEWS]
Your customers write better copy than you do. This prompt extracts it automatically.
Prompt 10 · Objection Crusher
Handle Every Sales Objection
I sell [PRODUCT/SERVICE] for [PRICE] to [AUDIENCE]. List the 5 most common objections my customers have before buying. For each objection: (1) State the objection as the customer would say it, (2) Write a 2–3 sentence response that addresses it without being defensive, (3) Suggest where in my sales process to place this response (sales page, FAQ, email sequence, etc.).
Add these responses to your FAQ section. Answering objections before they're raised is what separates 1% conversion pages from 5%+ pages.

Prompt 11 · SOP Builder
Turn Any Process Into a System
I'm going to describe how I currently do [TASK/PROCESS]. Your job: (1) Identify the 3 biggest time wasters in my current process, (2) Rewrite it as a numbered SOP (Standard Operating Procedure) with clear steps, (3) Flag which steps could be replaced or accelerated with AI tools, (4) Give me the exact prompt I should use for the AI-assisted steps. Here's my current process: [DESCRIBE YOUR PROCESS]
Use this on your top 3 recurring tasks first. Most creators waste 60% of their time on things AI can do in 30 seconds.
Prompt 12 · Email Automation
5-Email Welcome Sequence
Write a 5-email welcome sequence for new subscribers to my [NICHE] email list. Email 1: Welcome + deliver the freebie. Email 2 (Day 2): My story — why I started. Email 3 (Day 4): Best free resource I've made. Email 4 (Day 6): Introduce my paid product [PRODUCT NAME + PRICE]. Email 5 (Day 8): Soft close + FAQ. Tone: conversational, direct, no corporate fluff. Subject lines must be under 7 words.
Set this up once in MailerLite and it runs forever. Email 4 and 5 will generate passive sales on autopilot.
Prompt 13 · Content Recycler
Extract Maximum Value From One Video
Here is the transcript from my YouTube video: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT]. Extract and create: (1) 3 YouTube community post ideas, (2) 5 tweet-length insights (under 280 chars each), (3) 1 LinkedIn carousel outline (5 slides), (4) 1 email newsletter from the key takeaway, (5) 3 video title alternatives if I were to repurpose this as a new video. Format each section clearly.
One video should power 2 weeks of content across platforms. This prompt does it in under 5 minutes.
Prompt 14 · SEO Automation
Full SEO Package from One Topic
Give me a complete SEO package for a YouTube video about [TOPIC]. Include: (1) Primary keyword + 4 LSI keywords, (2) Optimized title (2 options — clickbait and search-optimized), (3) Description (first 150 chars hook + full 500-char description with keywords), (4) 15 tags, (5) 3 suggested chapters with timestamps. Target audience: [AUDIENCE].
Copy the description straight into YouTube. The chapter format gets featured snippets on Google.
Prompt 15 · Analytics Interpreter
Turn YouTube Stats Into Action
I'm going to share my YouTube video analytics. Analyze them and tell me: (1) What the CTR tells me about my packaging, (2) What the average view duration tells me about my content quality, (3) The single biggest lever to improve for my next video, (4) One hypothesis I should test in my next upload. Analytics: CTR [X%] · AVD [X:XX / X%] · Views [X] · Impressions [X] · Top traffic source [SOURCE].
Run this after every upload. Diagnosing what broke before moving on is how you compound channel growth.

Scale-Focused Income Systems
CAT 04
📈 From First Dollar to First $10K
Prompts built specifically to turn your content into revenue — not just views
Prompt 16 · Affiliate Strategy
Affiliate Stack Optimizer
I create content about [NICHE]. My audience is [AUDIENCE DESCRIPTION]. Recommend 6 affiliate programs I should promote, ranked by: (1) commission rate, (2) audience fit, (3) recurring vs one-time. For each: name the program, explain why it fits my audience, and give me the opening line I'd use to introduce it naturally in a video. Avoid programs that require 100K+ followers to get approved.
Recurring commissions are worth 5x a one-time payout over 12 months. Prioritize them every time.
Prompt 17 · Product Ladder
Build Your Full Income Stack
Based on my niche ([NICHE]) and audience ([AUDIENCE]), design a 4-tier product ladder: (1) Free lead magnet — what it is and what it teaches, (2) Low-ticket offer $7–$27 — what it is and how it's delivered, (3) Mid-ticket offer $97–$197 — format and promise, (4) High-ticket offer $497+ — what transformation it delivers. Make each tier a natural next step from the last.
You don't need all 4 tiers on day 1. Start with the freebie and the low-ticket. Add tiers as your audience grows.
Prompt 18 · Launch Sequence
7-Day Product Launch Email Campaign
Write a 7-day email launch sequence for my new product: [PRODUCT NAME] at [PRICE]. Structure: Day 1 — Tease (problem-focused), Day 2 — Story (how I built this), Day 3 — What's inside (features → benefits), Day 4 — Social proof (placeholder testimonials), Day 5 — Objections (address top 3), Day 6 — Urgency (closing tomorrow), Day 7 — Last chance + FAQ. Include subject lines for every email.
Day 6 and 7 typically drive 40–60% of launch revenue. Don't skip the urgency emails because they feel pushy.
Prompt 19 · Passive Income Audit
Find Hidden Money in Your Content
I've been creating content about [NICHE] for [TIME PERIOD]. Here's what I currently do: [LIST YOUR INCOME STREAMS OR "NONE YET"]. Audit my situation and identify: (1) The 3 fastest paths to my first $500/month from content, (2) One thing I'm likely leaving money on the table with right now, (3) The exact next step I should take this week. Be specific, not general.
Run this audit every 90 days. Your best income stream 6 months from now probably doesn't exist yet.
Prompt 20 · Pricing Psychology
Price Your Offer to Maximize Revenue
I'm pricing a digital product for [AUDIENCE] that delivers [TRANSFORMATION/OUTCOME]. It includes: [LIST WHAT'S INCLUDED]. Using pricing psychology principles (anchoring, charm pricing, value stacking), recommend: (1) The ideal price point, (2) How to frame the value stack to justify it, (3) A "most popular" tier if I were to offer 2–3 options, (4) The exact price display format ($XX vs $XX.00 vs "Just $XX").
Most creators underprice by 2–3x. The right price is the one that makes your best customers say "that's a no-brainer" — not "that's cheap."

Prompt 21 · Positioning Framework
The "Only I" Positioning Statement
Help me build an "Only I" positioning statement for my creator brand. My niche: [NICHE]. My background/experience: [YOUR STORY/SKILLS]. My audience: [WHO YOU HELP]. Complete this for me: "I'm the only creator who [UNIQUE METHOD] to help [AUDIENCE] achieve [OUTCOME] without [COMMON OBJECTION]." Give me 5 versions, ranked from weakest to strongest.
Put your strongest version in your YouTube About section and every social bio. Positioning is the most leveraged thing you can do — it does the selling before the sale.
Prompt 22 · Hook Engineering
The 3-Layer Hook System
Write 10 video hooks for a video about [TOPIC] targeting [AUDIENCE]. Use these 5 hook frameworks (2 each): (1) Counterintuitive statement ("Most people think X… but actually Y"), (2) Specific number hook ("I made $X in 30 days by doing only this"), (3) Mistake-based hook ("The biggest mistake I see creators make with X"), (4) Result-first hook (lead with the outcome, build to the method), (5) Story hook (single-sentence story that creates open loops). Label each hook with its framework.
Test 2 hooks as A/B thumbnails. The hook that wins as a title almost always wins as a thumbnail text too.
Prompt 23 · Audience Mirror
Talk Exactly Like Your Audience
I create content for [AUDIENCE] who want to [DESIRED OUTCOME]. Their biggest frustration is [MAIN PAIN POINT]. Write a 200-word "voice of customer" profile that describes: how they talk about their problem (in their own words), what words and phrases they use vs. avoid, their biggest dream outcome in 1 sentence, and the thing they've already tried that didn't work. I'll use this to write content that sounds like I'm reading their mind.
Paste this profile at the start of every content creation session. It forces AI to write for your actual audience, not a generic one.
Prompt 24 · Channel Strategy
The 90-Day Channel Growth Blueprint
I'm a YouTube creator in [NICHE] with [SUBSCRIBER COUNT] subscribers and [AVERAGE VIEWS/VIDEO] average views. My goal is to reach [SUBSCRIBER GOAL] in 90 days. Design a 90-day growth plan: (1) Weeks 1–4 strategy, (2) Weeks 5–8 strategy, (3) Weeks 9–12 strategy, (4) The single metric to focus on each phase, (5) What I should stop doing immediately. Be brutally honest about what the data suggests.
Re-run this every 90 days with updated stats. The plan changes as you grow — what works at 500 subs breaks at 5K.
Prompt 25 · The CFS Master Prompt
Your Personalized AI Creator Assistant
You are my personal AI creator assistant. Here's everything you need to know about me: Name: [NAME]. Channel/brand: [BRAND NAME]. Niche: [NICHE]. Target audience: [AUDIENCE]. Current stats: [SUBS/VIEWS/EMAIL LIST SIZE]. Income goal: [MONTHLY GOAL]. Biggest challenge right now: [MAIN STRUGGLE]. Products/offers: [WHAT YOU SELL OR "NONE YET"]. When I ask you questions, always factor in my niche, audience, and goals. Never give generic advice. Ask clarifying questions if you need more context. Start by analyzing my situation and giving me my single most important next move.
This is the crown jewel. Fill in your details once and save it. Start every strategy session with this prompt — the AI knows your whole situation and gives advice that actually fits your stage.
