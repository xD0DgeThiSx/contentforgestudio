HOW TO USE THIS TRACKER

Pick Tools You Trust. Create Useful Content. Track the Outcome.

Affiliate income compounds when you build it as a system, not a spam link strategy.

1
Pick a narrow audience

Creators, freelancers, side hustlers, agencies, YouTubers, local businesses. The narrower your audience, the higher your conversion rate on every tool you promote.

2
Choose tools that match the audience

Voice tools for YouTubers. Video tools for creators. Writing tools for freelancers. Do not promote a tool your audience will never use — trust is your most valuable asset.

3
Create helpful content first

Tutorials, comparisons, workflows, mistakes, and realistic use cases convert better than hype. Education IS the conversion. The affiliate link is the last 10% of the content.

4
Track every link

Know which content produced clicks, signups, and commissions. Use UTM parameters or platform dashboards. A link you cannot track is a leak in your funnel.

5
Review weekly and double down

Which tools got clicks? Which content got saves? Which affiliate produced revenue? Double the content on the top performer. Cut or pause the rest.

CONTENT PLANNING MATRIX

Six Content Angles Per Tool

Each affiliate tool can produce at least six pieces of content. Rotate through these angles to avoid repetition and test what converts.

Angle 01
Demo
Show the tool solving one specific problem in real time. No setup, no theory — just the tool doing the thing.
Example: "I generated a 60-second voiceover in ElevenLabs in under 2 minutes. Here's how."
Angle 02
Comparison
Compare tool A vs tool B for one audience or one use case. Be honest — trust converts better than hype.
Example: "ElevenLabs vs Murf AI for YouTube creators — which one should you use?"
Angle 03
Workflow
Show how the tool fits into a bigger system. The tool is one step; the workflow is the value.
Example: "Script → ElevenLabs voice → Seedance video → CapCut edit. Full Short in 30 minutes."
Angle 04
Mistake
Explain what beginners do wrong with this tool and how to fix it. Mistake content gets saved and shared.
Example: "The biggest Seedance mistake beginners make — and what to do instead."
Angle 05
Case Study
Show an example output, real result, or time saved. Specificity builds credibility. Numbers help.
Example: "I used Murf AI for 30 days. Here's what I made and what I'd do differently."
Angle 06
Resource Roundup
Mention the tool inside a starter stack, checklist, or free guide. Low friction, high trust. Works well as a CTA anchor.
Example: "My full free AI creator stack — everything I use, all free or low cost."
Rotation Rule

Never run the same angle twice in a row for the same tool. Rotate: Demo → Comparison → Workflow → Mistake → Case Study → Roundup → repeat. Six angles = six weeks of content per program.

WEEKLY REVIEW

Simple Metrics to Guide Your Next Move

Spend 15 minutes every Monday reviewing these. The data tells you where to double down.

METRIC 01
Which tool got the most clicks?
→ Create another piece of content for that tool this week.
METRIC 02
Which content got the most saves?
→ Remake it with a new angle or hook. Saves signal intent.
METRIC 03
Which affiliate produced revenue?
→ Build a dedicated tutorial or comparison video for that tool.
METRIC 04
Which content produced zero engagement?
→ Change the hook, not the topic. Hook is usually the issue.
METRIC 05
Which funnel step is leaking?
→ Low clicks = weak CTA. Low signups = weak landing page. Low opens = weak subject line.
METRIC 06
What did the comments ask about?
→ That's your next piece of content. Questions are free research.
