01Content Idea Generator
THE PROMPT
Give me 10 content post ideas for [platform] about [topic]. Mix tips, personal stories, client wins, and questions. Make each one scroll-stopping.
Best for: Planning a full week of content in 5 minutes. Replace [platform] with YouTube, LinkedIn, TikTok, or Instagram.
02Viral Hook Builder
THE PROMPT
Write 5 opening lines for a social post about [topic] that grab attention in the first 3 seconds. Use curiosity, contrast, and bold claims.
Best for: YouTube intros, Instagram captions, TikTok hooks. The first line is everything.
03Carousel Script Starter
THE PROMPT
Give me a 5-slide Instagram carousel script about [topic]. Include a hook slide, 3 value slides, and a strong CTA slide with a specific action.
Best for: Educational carousels that build authority and drive saves — the highest-engagement post type on Instagram.
04Thought Leadership Builder
THE PROMPT
Write a short LinkedIn post that positions me as an expert in [industry]. Include 1 key insight, 1 personal touch, and end with a question for discussion.
Best for: Building authority with your ideal client before they're ready to buy. Posts conversations before pitches.
05Personal Story Format
THE PROMPT
Help me turn this personal story into a social post. Include setup, moment of realization, and takeaway lesson. Make it relatable and honest — not polished.
Best for: Building genuine connection. Stories outperform tips in every algorithm right now.
06Viral Hook Idea Bank
THE PROMPT
Write 5 short viral-style hooks for [topic]. Each should make someone stop scrolling. Include a mix of curiosity, bold claims, and surprising contrarian takes.
Best for: Building a swipe file of hooks you can test across multiple platforms and formats.

07YouTube Video Outline
THE PROMPT
Create a complete YouTube video outline for a video titled "[title]". Include hook, intro promise, 4–5 named sections with key points, and a CTA close. Target length: [X] minutes.
Best for: Getting from blank page to full outline in under 2 minutes. Works for any niche.
08Video Hook Generator
THE PROMPT
Write 3 different 30-second video hooks for a YouTube video about [topic]. Each hook should challenge a false belief, show proof, and promise a clear payoff. No fluff.
Best for: The first 30 seconds of any video — the make-or-break window for retention.
09YouTube Title Generator
THE PROMPT
Give me 10 YouTube title options for a video about [topic]. Include titles using: free vs paid angle, specific number, timeframe, hidden secret angle, and false belief challenge.
Best for: Packaging videos before scripting. Title first, always — the packaging decides if anyone watches.
10Video Description SEO
THE PROMPT
Write a YouTube video description for a video about [topic]. Include: a 2-sentence hook, bullet list of what viewers learn, tools mentioned, and relevant hashtags. SEO-optimized.
Best for: Search discovery. A strong description gets your video found long after the upload date.
11YouTube Script — Intro
THE PROMPT
Write a 60-second YouTube video intro for a video titled "[title]". Open with proof or a bold claim, add an anti-hype line, then promise 3 specific things the viewer will know by the end.
Best for: Setting retention up in the first minute — the most critical window in any video.
12Shorts Script Generator
THE PROMPT
Write a 34-second YouTube Shorts script about [topic]. Structure: visual hook (no sound needed) → 2-line foreshadow → "but" complication → "therefore" payoff → abrupt end. No outro.
Best for: The proven Short formula. Abrupt ending forces rewatch — that's what drives 90%+ retention.

13Welcome Email
THE PROMPT
Write a warm welcome email for new subscribers to my [type of business] newsletter. Include what to expect, why they should stay subscribed, and one immediate value they can use today.
Best for: Email #1 in any welcome sequence. First impressions compound — this email sets the tone for everything that follows.
14Lead Magnet Delivery Email
THE PROMPT
Write a short email that delivers my freebie [describe it] with a thank-you, clear download instructions, and a teaser of what's coming next. Keep it under 150 words.
Best for: The email that goes out immediately after signup. Short, clear, delivers exactly what was promised.
15Landing Page Hero Copy
THE PROMPT
Write a bold hero section for a landing page selling [product/service]. Include: headline (benefit-first, under 10 words), subheadline (who it's for + outcome), and 3 bullet benefits. No fluff.
Best for: Any lead magnet page, product page, or sales page. Benefit-first always outperforms feature-first.
16Cold Outreach Email
THE PROMPT
Write a friendly cold outreach email to introduce my [service] to [target client]. Keep it under 100 words. Open with their pain, offer a specific solution, and end with one easy yes/no question.
Best for: Fiverr and direct client outreach. Short emails get replies. Long emails get ignored.
17Value Drop Email
THE PROMPT
Write a short value-packed email on [topic] that teaches 1 useful tip in under 200 words. End with a subtle link or CTA — but only after the value is delivered. Never lead with the pitch.
Best for: Weekly newsletters and affiliate promotions. Education IS the conversion.
18Newsletter Subject Lines
THE PROMPT
Give me 5 subject lines for a newsletter about [topic]. Vary the tone across: curiosity, boldness, humor, and value promise. No clickbait — each subject line must deliver what it implies.
Best for: A/B testing subject lines. The subject line is the thumbnail of your email — it decides everything.

Create a 3-step monetization roadmap for a creator with skills in [area] and an audience of [size/type]. Include: 1 fast offer, 1 scalable product, and 1 recurring income stream. Be specific.

BONUS

Power-Up Techniques

Add these to any prompt to get better output — instantly.

01 · ROLE
Set a Role
"Act as a [expert role] with 10+ years in [field]."
Shifts tone, depth, and vocabulary instantly.
02 · GOAL
State the Outcome
"The outcome I need is [result] for [who] because [why]."
One sentence of context eliminates 80% of generic responses.
03 · AUDIENCE
Name the Reader
"My audience is [level, industry, pain]. Write for them specifically."
Stops writing for everyone — which means writing for no one.
04 · FORMAT
Request Structure
"Format as [bullets / table / numbered steps]."
Structure is half the work — ask for it upfront.
05 · CRITIQUE
Run a Critique Pass
"Critique this draft. List 3 specific weaknesses and how to fix each."
AI reviewing its own work catches what takes you 20 minutes.
06 · VOICE
Match Your Voice
"Match the style of this sample: [paste 2–3 sentences you've written]."
Kills generic AI voice in one step.
